## The Game-Changer: Brown Butter

The single biggest improvement to any chocolate chip cookie is browning the butter. It adds a nutty, caramel-like depth that takes these cookies from good to absolutely incredible. Yes, it's an extra step, but I promise it's worth it.

**How to brown butter:**
1. Melt butter in a light-colored pan (so you can see the color change)
2. Stir constantly as it foams and bubbles
3. Watch for brown specks and a nutty aroma
4. Immediately transfer to a bowl to stop the cooking

## The Recipe

**Ingredients:**
- 1 cup (226g) unsalted butter, browned and cooled
- 1 cup (200g) brown sugar
- ¬Ω cup (100g) granulated sugar
- 2 large eggs + 1 egg yolk
- 2 tsp vanilla extract
- 2¬º cups (280g) all-purpose flour
- 1 tsp baking soda
- 1 tsp fine sea salt
- 1 cup (170g) dark chocolate chunks
- 1 cup (170g) milk chocolate chips
- Flaky sea salt for topping

**Instructions:**

1. **Brown the butter** and let it cool for 10 minutes
2. **Mix wet ingredients**: Whisk together browned butter and both sugars until combined. Add eggs, yolk, and vanilla.
3. **Combine dry ingredients**: In a separate bowl, whisk flour, baking soda, and salt
4. **Fold together**: Gently mix wet and dry ingredients until just combined
5. **Add chocolate**: Fold in both types of chocolate
6. **Chill**: Refrigerate dough for at least 2 hours (or overnight for best results)
7. **Bake**: Scoop dough into balls, place on lined baking sheet. Bake at 350¬∞F for 11-13 minutes
8. **Finish**: Immediately sprinkle with flaky sea salt

## Why This Recipe Works

**Brown butter**: Adds complex flavor and depth

**Two sugars**: Brown sugar for chewiness, white sugar for spread and crisp edges

**Extra egg yolk**: Creates a richer, more tender cookie

**Two chocolates**: Dark chocolate for intensity, milk chocolate for sweetness

**Cold dough**: Prevents overspreading and creates those coveted thick cookies

**Flaky salt**: Balances sweetness and elevates every bite

## The Secret to Perfect Texture

Here's the trick: slightly underbake them. When you pull them from the oven, they should look barely set in the middle. They'll continue cooking on the hot pan and firm up as they cool. This is what creates that perfect gooey center.

## My Testing Notes

I baked over 50 batches testing variables:
- **Melted vs. browned butter**: Brown butter wins every time
- **Chill time**: Minimum 2 hours, but 24 hours is ideal
- **Baking temp**: 350¬∞F gives the best texture
- **Cookie size**: 3 tablespoon scoops create the perfect size

## Make-Ahead Tips

The dough freezes beautifully! Scoop into balls, freeze on a tray, then transfer to a freezer bag. Bake straight from frozen, adding 1-2 minutes to the bake time. Freshly baked cookies whenever you want them? Yes, please.

## Variations to Try

Once you master the base recipe:

- **Triple chocolate**: Use cocoa powder in the dough
- **Nutty**: Add toasted pecans or walnuts
