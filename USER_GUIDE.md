## Creating a New Post

### Step-by-Step Guide

1. **Log in to your admin panel**

2. **Click "Blog Posts"** in the left sidebar

3. **Click the "New Blog Posts" button** at the top right

4. **Fill out your post:**

   **Title** (Required)
   Your post's headline. Example: "A Weekend in Barcelona"

   **Publish Date** (Required)
   Click the calendar icon and choose a date. Posts appear on your site sorted by this date.

   **Featured Image** (Required)
   The main photo for your post. Click "Choose an image", then drag a file or click "Upload". Wait for the upload to finish — you'll see it appear in the field.

   **Excerpt** (Required)
   A short 2–3 sentence summary. This appears on your homepage and in subscriber emails. Make it enticing!

   **Category** (Required)
   Choose: Travel, Baking, or Lifestyle.

   **Featured Post** (Optional)
   Toggle this on if you want the post to appear prominently at the top of your homepage. Use it for your best or most recent posts.

   **Body** (Required)
   Your main post content. Write as much as you like — see formatting tips below.

   **Gallery Images** (Optional)
   Add multiple photos for a gallery at the bottom of your post. Great for travel albums or step-by-step recipe photos.

   **Tags** (Optional)
   Keywords for your post. Example: "italy", "summer", "recipes". Click "Add tags" and type each one, pressing Enter after each.

5. **Save your post:**
   Click "Save" in the top right corner. This saves your work privately — **your post is not live yet.** You can keep editing, come back later, or close the browser and return tomorrow. Nothing will change on the live site.

6. **When you're ready to publish:**
   See the [Writing Workflow](#writing-workflow) section below for the exact steps.

## Formatting Your Post Content

### Basic Formatting

**Bold text:**
Highlight the text and click the **B** button in the toolbar.

**Italic text:**
Highlight the text and click the *I* button.

**Headings:**
Click where you want the heading, then use the dropdown (it usually says "Paragraph") and choose "Heading 2" for main sections or "Heading 3" for subsections.

**Lists:**
Click the bullet list button for a bulleted list, or the number list button for a numbered list. Press Enter for each new item.

**Links:**
Highlight the text you want to link, click the chain-link button, paste the URL, and click "Add".

**Quotes / pull quotes:**
Highlight text and click the quote button. Great for highlighting a key takeaway.

### Adding Images in Your Post Body

1. Place your cursor where you want the image
2. Click the image button in the toolbar
3. Upload your photo
4. It will appear inline in your post

Images automatically resize to look great on both phone and desktop.

## Image Tips

### Sizes That Work Best

- **Featured Image**: Landscape (horizontal) photo, at least 1200px wide
- **Gallery Images**: Try to keep them a similar shape to each other
- **Body Images**: Any size works

### Compress Before Uploading

Large files slow down your site. Before uploading any photo:

1. Go to [tinypng.com](https://tinypng.com)
2. Drag your image in
3. Download the compressed version
4. Upload that version to your blog

Try to keep images under 500KB each.

### Where to Find Free Photos

- Your own photos (always the best!)
- [Unsplash](https://unsplash.com) — free, high quality
- [Pexels](https://pexels.com) — free, large library

## Writing Workflow

This is the most important section to understand. Here's exactly how saving and publishing works now.

### The Four Stages of a Post

Every post moves through these stages before it goes live:

| Stage | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Draft** | You're still writing. Nothing is live. Saving in this stage is free and instant. |
| **In Review** | Optional — you've finished writing and want to mark it as ready to check over. |
| **Ready** | You've reviewed it and it's ready to publish. |
| **Published** | It's live on flourandflaneuse.com for the world to see. |

### How to Write and Publish a Post

**Step 1 — Write your post**
Fill in all the fields and click "Save". The post is saved as a draft. Nothing goes live. You can close the browser and come back any time — your work is saved.

**Step 2 — Keep editing until you're happy**
Every time you want to save your progress, click "Save". This never affects the live site.

**Step 3 — Mark it as Ready**
When you're happy with the post, look at the status indicator at the top of the editor. It says "Draft". Click it and change it to **"Ready"**.

**Step 4 — Publish**
Click **"Publish" → "Publish now"**.

After a minute or two, your post will appear live on flourandflaneuse.com.

### Why it works this way

Previously, every click of "Save" rebuilt the entire site — even for a tiny typo fix. Each rebuild uses a small amount of your hosting allowance. By separating saving from publishing, you can write and edit freely without any cost. Only publishing (Step 4) triggers a rebuild.

### After Publishing — How Long Until It's Live?

Usually 1–3 minutes. After clicking "Publish now":
1. Wait about 2 minutes
2. Open [flourandflaneuse.com](https://flourandflaneuse.com) in a new tab
3. Do a hard refresh: hold **Shift** and click the reload button
4. Your new post should be there

## Editing an Existing Post

1. Click **"Blog Posts"** in the sidebar
2. Find the post in the list and click it
3. Make your changes
4. Click **"Save"** — this saves your edits privately, nothing changes on the live site yet
5. When you're done editing, change the status to **"Ready"** and click **"Publish" → "Publish now"**
6. Wait 1–2 minutes and check the live site

## Deleting a Post

1. Open the post you want to delete
2. Click **"Delete entry"** (red button near the top)
3. Confirm the deletion

The post will disappear from the site after the next rebuild.

**Note**: Deleted posts cannot be recovered. If you're unsure, save as a draft instead of deleting.

## Pro Tips for Great Posts

### Writing

- **Start strong**: Hook readers in your first paragraph
- **Use headings**: Break long posts into sections with Heading 2 and Heading 3
- **Short paragraphs**: 2–4 sentences each — much easier to read
- **Tell stories**: Personal details make posts memorable
- **Be specific**: "a tiny café on Rue Cler" is more interesting than "a café in Paris"

### Images

- **More is better**: Use photos throughout the post, not just at the top
- **Vary your shots**: Mix landscape views, close-ups, and detail shots
- **Show yourself**: People connect with faces
- **Caption wisely**: Use the text around images to add context

### Categories and Tags

- **Categories**: One per post — Travel, Baking, or Lifestyle
- **Tags**: 3–5 relevant keywords per post
- **Consistent tagging**: Use the same tags across related posts so readers can find them

### Featured Posts

- Update your featured posts when you publish something new you're proud of
- Feature timely content — holiday recipes in December, summer travel in June

## Common Questions

### Can I schedule a post to go live on a specific day?

Not automatically. The workaround:
1. Write and save your post as a draft
2. Set the Publish Date to the day you want
3. On that day, change the status to "Ready" and click "Publish"

### What if I accidentally close the browser while writing?

Your draft is saved to the CMS as long as you clicked "Save" at least once. Just go back to the admin panel and find the post in your drafts.

### Can I undo changes to a post?

Not through the editor itself. If you've accidentally changed something and haven't published yet, you can leave without saving. If you've already published, you'll need to edit it back manually.

### Will old links still work if I edit a post?

Yes. Editing a post never changes its URL — the link is based on the post's original title and date.

### Can I have multiple authors?

Yes — contact Rayaan and he can send an invitation to any additional editor through the Netlify dashboard.

## Troubleshooting

### Can't log in?

- Double-check your email and password
- Click "Forgot password" and follow the reset email
- Check that you accepted the original invitation email
- Try clearing your browser's cookies and trying again

### My published post isn't showing on the site yet

- Wait 2–3 minutes — the site takes a moment to rebuild after publishing
- Do a hard refresh: hold **Shift** and click reload (or Cmd + Shift + R on Mac)
- Make sure you clicked **"Publish" → "Publish now"**, not just "Save"
- Check that the post status says **"Published"** (not "Draft" or "Ready")

### My edits aren't showing up

The same as above — make sure you completed all the way to "Publish now", not just "Save". Saving alone never changes the live site.

### Image not uploading?

- Check your internet connection
- Make sure the file is under 5MB (compress at tinypng.com first)
- Use JPG or PNG format only
- Try a different browser if it keeps failing

### Got an error message?

- Take a screenshot of the message
- Try logging out and back in
- If it keeps happening, contact Rayaan
